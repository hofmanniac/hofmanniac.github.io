# This Is Fine: Space Edition

So I've been working on this board game idea that perfectly captures the experience of... well, trying to hold anything together in 2026, really.

It's called **This Is Fine: Space Edition**, and it's exactly what it sounds like: a game about building and managing a spaceship that's perpetually one bad decision away from catastrophic failure.

## The Concept

Picture this: You're adding rooms to your ship like you're playing space Tetris. You're moving around a shared galaxy map. Events keep happening - fires, malfunctions, weird discoveries that may or may not explode. Everything is stacking up in different rooms, and you have *just enough* actions each turn to fix *most* of the problems.

Most.

The game asks the eternal question: "Do I deal with this now, or can I ignore it for one more turn?"

(Spoiler: It spreads.)

## The Vibe

The whole thing leans into stick-figure art and "this is fine" energy. It's strategic chaos with a sense of humor. You're building your ridiculous ship, completing missions, exploring the galaxy, and occasionally helping other playersâ€”or watching them burn while you steal their cargo. It's flexible that way.

The goal is to score points through missions before your ship becomes a floating disaster. Or I guess technically *more* of a floating disaster than it already is.

## Current Status

Still very much a work-in-progress! I'm playtesting mechanics, refining the event system, and trying to figure out exactly how mean the galaxy should be allowed to be.
